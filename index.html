    DBT Skills Definitions

    🧘‍♂️ Mindfulness – Observe, Describe, Participate in the moment without judgment.

    ⚡ Distress Tolerance – TIP (Temperature, Intense exercise, Paced breathing, Progressive relaxation), ACCEPTS (Activities, Contributing, Comparisons, Emotions, Pushing away, Thoughts, Sensations), and Self-Soothe (use the five senses to calm yourself).

    🌊 Emotion Regulation – Check the Facts (do your emotions fit the facts?), Opposite Action (act opposite to unhelpful emotions).

    🤝 Interpersonal Effectiveness – DEAR MAN (assert needs), GIVE (relationship skills), FAST (self-respect effectiveness).

    CBT Skills Definitions

    🔹 Antecedent – The event or situation that triggers a thought, emotion, or behavior. Tip: Identify triggers early to plan coping strategies.

    💭 Automatic Thought – Immediate, often subconscious thoughts in response to an antecedent. Tip: Pause and label these thoughts before reacting.

    🧠 Cognitive Distortion – Patterns of thinking that are biased or unhelpful. Tip: Recognize distortions to challenge and reframe them.

    ⚫ All-or-Nothing Thinking – Seeing things in black and white. Tip: Look for middle-ground possibilities.

    🔄 Overgeneralization – Drawing broad conclusions from a single event. Tip: Ask yourself: “Is this always true?”

    🌓 Mental Filter – Focusing only on the negative. Tip: Make a list of positive events to balance perspective.

    🌪 Catastrophizing – Expecting the worst-case scenario. Tip: Evaluate likelihood and prepare realistic plans.

    🙋 Personalization – Taking responsibility for events out of your control. Tip: Ask yourself: “Whose responsibility is this really?”

    🏃 Behavioral Activation – Engage in activities that bring accomplishment or pleasure. Tip: Schedule at least one positive activity daily.

    📓 Thought Record – Tool to identify and challenge automatic thoughts. Tip: Write down the evidence for and against your thoughts.

    💎 Core Beliefs – Deeply held assumptions about yourself, others, or the world. Tip: Examine if beliefs are flexible or rigid.

    🚪 Exposure – Gradually facing feared situations or thoughts. Tip: Start small, increase exposure gradually, track progress.

    Coping Mechanisms & Tips

    🛑 Grounding Techniques – Use senses to reconnect with the present. Tip: Try “5-4-3-2-1” (see, touch, hear, smell, taste).

    🤔 Thought Challenging – Question whether automatic thoughts are true. Tip: Ask: “What’s the evidence for this thought?”

    📅 Activity Scheduling – Plan meaningful or pleasurable activities. Tip: Treat them as non-negotiable appointments.

    💆 Relaxation Techniques – Deep breathing, progressive muscle relaxation, or guided imagery. Tip: Practice daily for short sessions to build habit.

    🛠 Problem-Solving – Break problems into smaller steps. Tip: Brainstorm multiple solutions, then test the most realistic one.

    📊 Self-Monitoring – Track moods, thoughts, and behaviors. Tip: Journaling 5–10 minutes daily can reveal patterns.

    💬 Positive Self-Talk – Replace negative internal dialogue with supportive statements. Tip: Prepare a list of 3–5 go-to affirmations.

    🧪 Behavior Experiments – Test beliefs or assumptions in real life. Tip: Treat failures as data, not personal flaws.
